1
 [Psalm 46] A song of confidence in
God's protection of Zion with close parallels to Psalm 48. The dominant note in
Psalm 46 is sounded by the refrain, The LORD of hosts is with us
(
⇒
 Psalm 46:4, 8,
⇒
 12). The first strophe (
⇒
 Psalm
46:2-4) sings of the security of God's presence even in utter chaos;
the second (
⇒
 Psalm 46:5-8), of divine protection of
the city Psalm 46 from its enemies; the third (
⇒
 Psalm
46:9-11), of God's imposition of imperial peace.
2
 [1] Alamoth: the melody of the
psalm, now lost. 
3
 [3-4] Figurative ancient Near
Eastern language to describe social and political upheavals.
4
 [4] The first line of the refrain
is similar in structure and meaning to Isaiah's name for the royal child,
Immanuel, With us is God (

 Isaiah 7:14;
⇒
 8:8, 
⇒
 10).
