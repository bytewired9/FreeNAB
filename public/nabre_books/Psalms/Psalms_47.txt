All you peoples, clap your hands; shout to God
with joyful cries. 
3
For the LORD, the Most High, inspires awe, the
great king overall the earth, 
4

under our feet, 
5
[2]
 Who chose a land for our heritage, the glory of

6
[3]
 God mounts the throne amid shouts of joy; the
LORD, amid trumpet blasts. 
7
Sing praise to God, sing praise; sing praise to
our king, sing praise. 
8
God is king over all the earth; sing hymns of
praise. 
9
God rules over the nations; God sits upon his
holy throne. 
10
The princes of the peoples assemble with the

enthroned on high. 
1
 [Psalm 47] A hymn calling on the
nations to acknowledge the universal rule of Israel's God
(
⇒
 Psalm 47:2-5) who is enthroned as king over
Israel and the nations (
⇒
 Psalm 47:6-9). 
2
 [5] Our heritage . . . the glory:
the land of Israel (cf 
⇒
 Isaiah 58:14), which God
has given Israel in an act of sovereignty.
3
 [6] God mounts the throne:
"has gone up to the throne," according to the context (
⇒
 Psalm
47:9). Christian liturgical tradition has applied the verse to the
ascension of Christ.
